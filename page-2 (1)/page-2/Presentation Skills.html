Presentation Skills

Creating a summary for presentation skills involves condensing the main ideas into a shorter form using your own words. This technique is appropriate when the primary concepts are relevant or when complex information needs to be simplified into key points for your audience. When summarizing, ensure accuracy and completeness, and distinguish it from paraphrasing, which requires rephrasing a passage while maintaining the original meaning and length. Direct quotes can be used sparingly for impact, retaining the source's wording, sentence structure, and punctuation exactly. In presentations, use source material cautiously on slides, keeping it concise and avoiding lengthy quotes or paraphrases. Citations are necessary for paraphrases, summaries, and quotes on slides, often presented in smaller text. Adhere to these guidelines to effectively incorporate sources into your presentations without plagiarizing or relying excessively on direct quotations.
